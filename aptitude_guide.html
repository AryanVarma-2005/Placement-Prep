#02

Time & Work

How long does it take multiple workers to finish a job?

Core Idea

If a person completes a job in N days, their work rate (work done per day) is 1/N. When multiple people work together, their rates ADD up.

Key Formulas
Work Rate = 1 / Days_to_complete
Combined Rate (A+B) = 1/A + 1/B
Days together = 1 / (1/A + 1/B) = (A×B) / (A+B)
Work done = Rate × Time
Example

A can do a job in 10 days, B in 15 days. How long together?

A's rate = 1/10, B's rate = 1/15
Combined = 1/10 + 1/15 = 3/30 + 2/30 = 5/30 = 1/6
∴ Together = 6 days ✓
⚡ Shortcut

For two people: Days together = (A × B) / (A + B). For the above: (10×15)/(10+15) = 150/25 = 6 days.

Pipes & Cistern (same concept)

Inlet pipe fills in A hours → rate = +1/A. Outlet pipe empties in B hours → rate = −1/B. Net rate = 1/A − 1/B.

Time to fill = 1 / (1/A − 1/B)

Partial Work / MDH Rule

M men finish work in D days working H hours/day. Then M × D × H = Total Work (constant).

M₁ × D₁ × H₁ = M₂ × D₂ × H₂
#03

Time & Distance

Speed, distance, and the golden triangle relationship.

The Golden Triangle

Core Relationship
Speed = Distance / Time
Distance = Speed × Time
Time = Distance / Speed

Unit Conversion: 1 km/hr = 5/18 m/s | 1 m/s = 18/5 km/hr

Average Speed

When same DISTANCE is covered at two different speeds:

Avg Speed = 2×S₁×S₂ / (S₁+S₂)
⚡ Warning

Do NOT simply average the speeds! Use the harmonic mean formula above.

Relative Speed

Same direction: |S₁ − S₂|
Opposite direction: S₁ + S₂

Used in problems: trains crossing each other, catching up, etc.

Train Problems

Train crossing a pole / person
Time = Length of train / Speed of train

Train crossing a platform / bridge
Time = (Length of train + Length of platform) / Speed

Two trains crossing each other
Time = (L₁ + L₂) / Relative Speed
#04

Boats & Streams

Upstream vs downstream — how current affects speed.

Key Terms & Formulas

Still Water Speed (u) = boat's speed without current. Stream Speed (v) = current speed.

Effective Speeds
Downstream (with current) = u + v
Upstream (against current) = u − v

Finding u and v from speeds
u (boat speed) = (Downstream + Upstream) / 2
v (stream speed) = (Downstream − Upstream) / 2
Example

A boat goes 30 km downstream in 3 hrs and 20 km upstream in 4 hrs. Find boat & stream speed.

Downstream speed = 30/3 = 10 km/hr
Upstream speed = 20/4 = 5 km/hr
Boat speed = (10+5)/2 = 7.5 km/hr
Stream speed = (10−5)/2 = 2.5 km/hr ✓
#05

Ratio & Proportion

Comparing quantities and scaling them correctly.

Ratio Basics

A ratio a:b means for every 'a' parts of one quantity, there are 'b' parts of another.

Proportion — Four quantities a, b, c, d
a:b = c:d → a×d = b×c (cross multiplication)

Mean proportion of a, b: x = √(a×b)
Third proportion to a, b: x = b²/a

Compound Ratio

Multiply the ratios: a:b and c:d → Compound = ac:bd

Example

Compound ratio of 2:3 and 4:5 = 8:15

Alligation / Mixture

Mix two items at prices p₁ and p₂ to get a mean price M:

Ratio = (p₂−M) : (M−p₁)
#06

Problems on Age

Setting up equations from verbal age relationships.

Strategy: Always Use Variables

Let current age = x. Past/future ages shift by a constant: Past age = x − n, Future age = x + n.

Standard Setup
Current age = x
Age n years ago = x − n
Age n years later = x + n
If ratio of ages is a:b → ages = ak, bk for some k
Example

The ratio of A's age to B's age is 3:5. After 10 years, the ratio becomes 5:7. Find their ages.

Let ages = 3k and 5k
After 10 yrs: (3k+10)/(5k+10) = 5/7
7(3k+10) = 5(5k+10)
21k + 70 = 25k + 50
4k = 20 → k = 5
A = 15, B = 25 ✓
⚡ Key Insight

The DIFFERENCE between two people's ages is always constant, no matter what year you're in. Use this to simplify equations!

#08

Permutation & Combination

Counting arrangements vs. selections.

The Core Difference

Permutation (P) = ORDER matters (arrangements). Combination (C) = order does NOT matter (selections/groups).

Fundamental Formulas
P(n,r) = n! / (n−r)! ← r items from n, ordered
C(n,r) = n! / [r! × (n−r)!] ← r items from n, unordered
Relation: P(n,r) = C(n,r) × r!

n! = n × (n−1) × (n−2) × ... × 1
0! = 1 (by definition)
Example

In how many ways can 3 students be selected from 5 for a team (no order), and arranged in a line (order matters)?

Select (no order) = C(5,3) = 5!/(3!×2!) = 10 ways
Arrange (order matters) = P(5,3) = 5!/2! = 60 ways ✓

Circular Permutation

Arranging n items in a circle:

Circular P = (n−1)!

Because one position is fixed as reference, reducing arrangements by factor n.

Repetition Allowed

Fill r positions from n items
(repetition allowed) = nʳ
⚡ Trick

PIN of 4 digits from 0–9 = 10⁴ = 10,000 possibilities (with repetition).

Probability

How likely is an event to happen?

Foundational Formula

P(Event) = Favorable Outcomes / Total Outcomes

P(A') [not A] = 1 − P(A)
P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) − P(A and B)
P(A and B) [independent] = P(A) × P(B)

0 ≤ P(E) ≤ 1 always

Standard Reference Values

ExperimentSample SpaceTotal
Coin tossH, T2
Two coinsHH, HT, TH, TT4
Dice roll1,2,3,4,5,66
Two dice(1,1)…(6,6)36
Deck of cards52 cards52
Example

A card is drawn from a deck. Find P(King or Heart).

P(King) = 4/52, P(Heart) = 13/52
P(King of Heart) = 1/52 (both)
P(King or Heart) = 4/52 + 13/52 − 1/52 = 16/52 = 4/13 ✓
#10

Simple Interest

Interest calculated only on the original principal.

Core Formulas

SI = (P × R × T) / 100
Amount (A) = P + SI = P(1 + RT/100)

P = Principal, R = Rate % per year, T = Time in years

P = 100 × SI / (R × T)
R = 100 × SI / (P × T)
T = 100 × SI / (P × R)
Example

Find SI on ₹5000 at 8% per annum for 3 years. Also find total Amount.

SI = (5000 × 8 × 3) / 100 = 1200
Amount = 5000 + 1200 = ₹6200 ✓
#11

Compound Interest

Interest calculated on principal AND previously earned interest.

Core Formulas

Amount (A) = P × (1 + R/100)ᵀ
CI = A − P = P[(1+R/100)ᵀ − 1]

Compounded n times per year
A = P × (1 + R/n×100)ⁿᵀ

Half-yearly
A = P × (1 + R/200)²ᵀ
⚡ CI vs SI (2 years)

CI − SI (2 yrs) = P(R/100)²
This is a handy formula for quick calculation.

Example

₹10,000 at 10% p.a. for 2 years CI.

A = 10000×(1.1)² = ₹12,100
CI = ₹2,100

#12–13

Clocks

Angles between hands and coincidences in a 12-hour clock.

Speed of Clock Hands

Minute hand speed: 360° in 60 min = 6°/min
Hour hand speed: 360° in 720 min = 0.5°/min
Relative speed of minute hand: 6 − 0.5 = 5.5°/min

Angle between hands at H hrs M mins:
θ = |30H − 5.5M| (take the smaller angle if > 180°)
Example

Find angle between clock hands at 3:30.

θ = |30×3 − 5.5×30| = |90 − 165| = 75° ✓

Hands Overlap (Coincide)

Hands overlap every 65 5/11 minutes. They overlap 22 times in 24 hours.

Coincidence after H o'clock:
M = 60H/11 minutes

Hands at Right Angles (90°)

Hands form 90° every 32 8/11 minutes. Happens 44 times in 24 hours.

Time for 90°: M = (60H ± 180)/11
#16–17

Calendar

Finding the day of the week for any date.

Odd Days Concept

Divide total days by 7. The remainder = "odd days". This tells you the day of the week (0=Sun, 1=Mon, … 6=Sat).

Ordinary year: 365 days = 52 weeks + 1 odd day
Leap year: 366 days = 52 weeks + 2 odd days

Leap year rule:
→ Divisible by 4? → Leap (usually)
→ Century year (100,200…): ONLY if divisible by 400
(e.g., 1900 is NOT a leap year, 2000 IS)

Odd Days in Centuries

CenturyOdd DaysDay
100 years5Friday
200 years3Wednesday
300 years1Monday
400 years0Sunday
⚡ Same Calendar Year

Two years have the same calendar if they start on the same day AND are both ordinary OR both leap years. The cycle repeats every 28 years for non-century boundaries.

#18

Direction Sense Test

Tracking movement on a compass grid.

The Compass & Turns

Facing North, turn Right → now facing East
Facing North, turn Left → now facing West
Turn 180° (about turn) → reverse direction

Shortest distance (straight line):
√(horizontal displacement² + vertical displacement²)
⚡ Method

Draw it! Always sketch the path on paper. Assign N=up, S=down, E=right, W=left. Track x (East-West) and y (North-South) separately, then use Pythagoras for final distance.

Example

Ravi walks 6km North, turns right and walks 8km. Find distance from start.

North = 6 km (vertical), East = 8 km (horizontal)
Distance = √(6² + 8²) = √(36+64) = √100 = 10 km ✓

#20

Water & Mirror Images

How reflections transform figures and letters.

Mirror Image (Left-Right flip)

Mirror is placed vertically to the right or left of the object. Image = left and right are swapped. UP and DOWN stay the same.

Mirror image letters:
A,H,I,M,O,T,U,V,W,X,Y
← These look same in mirror!

Water Image (Up-Down flip)

Mirror is placed horizontally below the object. Image = top and bottom are swapped. Left and RIGHT stay the same.

Water image letters:
B,C,D,E,H,I,K,O,X
← These look same in water!
⚡ Clock in Mirror

Time shown in mirror = 11:60 − actual time. If clock shows 8:20 in mirror, real time = 11:60 − 8:20 = 3:40.

#23

Venn Diagram

Visualizing overlapping sets and their counts.

Key Formulas

Two Sets A and B
n(A∪B) = n(A) + n(B) − n(A∩B)
Only A = n(A) − n(A∩B)
Only B = n(B) − n(A∩B)

Three Sets A, B, C
n(A∪B∪C) = n(A)+n(B)+n(C) − n(A∩B) − n(B∩C) − n(A∩C) + n(A∩B∩C)
Example

In a class of 50: 30 like Maths, 25 like Science, 15 like both. How many like at least one?

n(M∪S) = 30 + 25 − 15 = 40
Only Maths = 30 − 15 = 15
Only Science = 25 − 15 = 10
Neither = 50 − 40 = 10 ✓
#24

Syllogism

Drawing valid conclusions from given statements.

Types of Statements

TypeStatementVenn Diagram
Universal Positive (A)All A are BCircle A inside Circle B
Universal Negative (E)No A is BCircles A and B separate
Particular Positive (I)Some A are BCircles A and B overlap
Particular Negative (O)Some A are not BA partially outside B
⚡ Key Rules

1. "All + All = All" — All A→B, All B→C ∴ All A→C.   2. "All + No = No" — All A→B, No B→C ∴ No A→C.   3. "Some + All = Some" — Some A→B, All B→C ∴ Some A→C.   4. Two particular premises give NO definite conclusion.
